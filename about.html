Mental Health

A person struggling with mental health issues may neglect their physical health or damage their bodies. Mental health problems may lead to physical heath problems as a result of symptoms such as cu**ing yourself, punching the wall until your knuckles bleed, hitting something really hard with brute force. Please dont make fun of people who do these, instead, talk to them or advise them to go to the school councillor. This is an actual problem and shouldn't be made fun of

More than 1 in 7 people in Singapore have mental health issues. Su*c*de is the third leading cause of d*ath among teens because of the stress and pressure they go through from everyone, including themselves.
Mental illness has a wide range from mild to very severe.

The most common mental illnesses in teens are:

  • Generalized Anxiety — Excessive worry about everyday matters
  • Social Phobias // Social Anxiety Disorder — Severe feelings of self-consciousness and insecurity in social settings
  • Depression — Persistent feelings of sadness, anxiety, and/or emptiness changes in sleep

Signs to look out for

Even if you have 5 out of 7 signs, you can’t self diagnose.
You have to be clinically diagnosed by a psychiatrist.

  • Changes in sleep
  • New onset of guilt
  • Changes in energy level
  • Changes in concentration or task completion
  • Changes in appetite
  • Changes in motivation
  • Thoughts of su*c*de

generalized anxiety disorder

  • Feeling restless, wound up, or on edge
  • Becoming fatigued easily
  • Struggling to concentrate
  • Experiencing irritation
  • Feeling muscle tension
  • Having difficulty keeping worry levels under control
  • Struggling with sleep, such as difficulties falling asleep or staying asleep, or not feeling well-rested

social phobia // social anxiety disorder

  • Feeling very anxious at the thought of being around others, and struggling to talk to other people
  • Experiencing extreme self-consciousness and fear of humiliation, embarrassment, rejection, or offending people
  • Worrying about being judged
  • Feeling anxious days or even weeks ahead of a social event
  • Avoiding places where other people will be
  • Struggling to make and / or keep friends
  • Blushing, sweating, or trembling around others
  • Experiencing nausea around other people

depression

  • Feeling persistently sad, anxious, or empty
  • Experiencing hopelessness or pessimism
  • Struggling with irritation
  • Feeling guilty, worthless, or helpless
  • Losing interest in hobbies or activities that used to be enjoyable
  • Fatigue or lack of energy
  • Moving // talking more slowly than usual
  • Feeling restless
  • Struggling with concentration, memory, and/or decision-making
  • Experiencing unexplained changes in appetite or weight
  • Having thoughts of d*ath or su*c*de
  • Unexplained aching or pains that don’t go away even when treated
