PTSD

Post-traumatic stress disorder (commonly known as PTSD) is a mental health condition that is triggered by a horrifying or distressing event, either experiencing or witnessing it. Similar events prior to the trauma can trigger PTSD as well.

Symptoms

  • Reoccurring, disturbing, and unwanted memories of the event
  • Reliving the event (Having flashbacks)
  • Experiencing nightmares of the event
  • Severe mental and physical distress to something that is similar or reminds you of the event
  • Negative thoughts about everything
  • Hopelessness and lack of interest in anything
  • Being easily startled, even by small things
  • Having trouble sleeping or concentrating

How it affects their life

  • kidney failure
  • heart problems
  • digestive issues or constipation

How it affects their life

People who suffer from Post-traumatic stress disorder may start to feel negative about life after witnessing or experiencing events that are very disturbing. They may feel detached from family, friends, or from the world. People with PTSD may lose important friends or family members because they shut themselves away from reality due to the disorder. They lose sleep and are unable to live properly without thinking about the trauma they have experienced.
